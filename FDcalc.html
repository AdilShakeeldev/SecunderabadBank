About Fixed Deposits

Fixed deposits are one of the most popular investment options that offer guaranteed returns. They provide a safe and secure way to grow your savings with predictable interest rates.

Key Benefits

  • Guaranteed returns with fixed interest rates
  • Flexible tenure options from days to years
  • Higher interest rates compared to regular savings
  • Option for regular interest payouts or compounding
  • Safe investment backed by deposit insurance

How to Use This Calculator

Adjust the sliders to set your principal amount, interest rate, and tenure. Choose your preferred compounding frequency to see how your investment will grow over time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about fixed deposits.

A fixed deposit is a financial instrument provided by banks which provides investors a higher rate of interest than a regular savings account, until the given maturity date. It may or may not require the creation of a separate account.

Interest on fixed deposits is calculated using the compound interest formula. The frequency of compounding (monthly, quarterly, half-yearly, or yearly) affects the final returns. The more frequent the compounding, the higher the returns.

Yes, most banks allow premature withdrawal of fixed deposits. However, this usually comes with a penalty in the form of a reduced interest rate. The exact penalty varies from bank to bank.

Yes, the interest earned on fixed deposits is taxable as per your income tax slab. TDS is deducted if the interest earned exceeds ₹40,000 per year (₹50,000 for senior citizens). You can submit Form 15G/15H to avoid TDS if you're eligible.
